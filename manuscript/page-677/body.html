He knew, or he could imagine, the arguments which proved his own nonexistence; but they were nonsense, they were only a play on words.

Did not the statement, 'You do not exist', contain a logical absurdity? But what use was it to say so? His mind shrivelled as he thought of the unanswerable, mad arguments with which O'Brien would demolish him. 'I think I exist,' he said wearily.
